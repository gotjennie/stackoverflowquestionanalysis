V<p>Lets start with three arrays of <code>dtype=np.double</code>. Timings are performed on a intel CPU using numpy 1.7.1 compiled with <code>icc</code> and linked to intel's <code>mkl</code>. A AMD cpu with numpy 1.6.1 compiled with <code>gcc</code> without <code>mkl</code> was also used to verify the timings. Please note the timings scale nearly linearly with system size and are not due to the small overhead incurred in the numpy functions <code>if</code> statements these difference will show up in microseconds not milliseconds:</p>\u000a\u000a<pre><code>arr_1D=np.arange(500,dtype=np.double)\u000alarge_arr_1D=np.arange(100000,dtype=np.double)\u000aarr_2D=np.arange(500**2,dtype=np.double).reshape(500,500)\u000aarr_3D=np.arange(500**3,dtype=np.double).reshape(500,500,500)\u000a</code></pre>\u000a\u000a<p>First lets look at the <code>np.sum</code> function:</p>\u000a\u000a<pre><code>np.all(np.sum(arr_3D)==np.einsum('ijk-&gt;',arr_3D))\u000aTrue\u000a\u000a%timeit np.sum(arr_3D)\u000a10 loops, best of 3: 142 ms per loop\u000a\u000a%timeit np.einsum('ijk-&gt;', arr_3D)\u000a10 loops, best of 3: 70.2 ms per loop\u000a</code></pre>\u000a\u000a<p>Powers:</p>\u000a\u000a<pre><code>np.allclose(arr_3D*arr_3D*arr_3D,np.einsum('ijk,ijk,ijk-&gt;ijk',arr_3D,arr_3D,arr_3D))\u000aTrue\u000a\u000a%timeit arr_3D*arr_3D*arr_3D\u000a1 loops, best of 3: 1.32 s per loop\u000a\u000a%timeit np.einsum('ijk,ijk,ijk-&gt;ijk', arr_3D, arr_3D, arr_3D)\u000a1 loops, best of 3: 694 ms per loop\u000a</code></pre>\u000a\u000a<p>Outer product:</p>\u000a\u000a<pre><code>np.all(np.outer(arr_1D,arr_1D)==np.einsum('i,k-&gt;ik',arr_1D,arr_1D))\u000aTrue\u000a\u000a%timeit np.outer(arr_1D, arr_1D)\u000a1000 loops, best of 3: 411 us per loop\u000a\u000a%timeit np.einsum('i,k-&gt;ik', arr_1D, arr_1D)\u000a1000 loops, best of 3: 245 us per loop\u000a</code></pre>\u000a\u000a<p>All of the above are twice as fast with <code>np.einsum</code>.
